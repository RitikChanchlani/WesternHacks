As our world becomes more digitally connected, children around the world have started to connect through cyberspace. With the COVID-19 pandemic, children have begun to rely on online communication to connect with their peers. With more children communicating online everyday it is important that they understand the consequences of cyber Bullying and that when communicating online, misinterpretations of texts and emails can result in accidental cyberbullying. For this reason, we created stopcyberbullying.com to educate students and give them resources to check their messages BEFORE they are sent.

Image By:Martins Ratkus

Our website contains two different tools that help in the fight against cyberbullying. Our first tool allows users to check if their messages are potentially harmful or contain any homophobic, racist, sexist, and sexual content. It takes a message and alerts the user which harmful words it contains, catching a variety of loopholes such as slight variations in obscene words, the usage of negation to create a negative message, and many more. Highlighting negative keywords allows users to fully comprehend the negativity of their language, helping users realize and improve their diction. Our second tool allows users to check if a website potentially contains harmful language, using the same screening system as the first tool. The user can input a URL and the tool will tell the user a list of harmful and potentially concerning words on the website. This tool is especially useful for parents, allowing them to check what websites their child visits, before visiting them. This allows parents to filter out pages with large amounts of negative content, helping keep their children sheltered and safe.
